## Private payments, without asking users to become cryptographers

SPECTER gives you a fresh **stealth address** for every payment.

That means a sender can pay you without exposing your main wallet to everyone watching the chain.

The privacy layer is built with **ML-KEM-768**, the finalized post-quantum key encapsulation standard used in the codebase through RustCrypto `ml-kem` (`specter/specter-crypto/src/kyber.rs`).

## What problem does SPECTER solve?

On a normal public chain, your address becomes your identity.

Once someone knows your address, they can often follow your incoming payments, your balances, and your counterparties.

SPECTER changes the receiving side of that experience:

1. You publish one public **meta-address**.
2. Every sender derives a different one-time **stealth address** from it.
3. The sender posts an **announcement** that only you can decode.
4. You scan announcements and recover the payment meant for you.

## The full story in one screen

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For users">
    You do not hand out one public wallet forever.

    You publish a meta-address once, and SPECTER turns it into a new receiving destination each time.

    The sender never needs your real wallet address.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="For builders">
    The core API loop is:

    - `POST https://backend.specterpq.com/api/v1/keys/generate`
    - `POST https://backend.specterpq.com/api/v1/stealth/create`
    - `POST https://backend.specterpq.com/api/v1/registry/announcements`
    - `POST https://backend.specterpq.com/api/v1/stealth/scan`

    That flow maps directly to `specter/specter-api/src/handlers.rs`.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="For researchers">
    SPECTER already uses ML-KEM-768 for recipient discovery and announcement privacy, but the current wallet-compatible spend path still derives a `secp256k1` key (`specter/specter-crypto/src/derive.rs`).

    That is why the docs now separate **post-quantum recipient privacy** from **classical spend security**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Why this matters

| Question | Short answer |
| --- | --- |
| Can people watch the chain and see a payment happened? | Yes. |
| Can they easily link every payment back to your public receiving identity? | SPECTER is designed to make that much harder. |
| Is the receiving privacy layer post-quantum? | Yes, for the ML-KEM announcement/discovery path. |
| Is the current spend path fully post-quantum? | No. The spendable wallet key remains `secp256k1` today. |
